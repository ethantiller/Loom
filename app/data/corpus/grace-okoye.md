# Grace Okoye

Grace Okoye is a cybersecurity entrepreneur who founded Pinnacle Security after
beginning her engineering career at Beacon Semiconductor.

Okoye joined Beacon Semiconductor in the mid-2000s and worked directly under Leon
Ashby on hardware validation and security architecture for the Photon chip. Her years
at Beacon gave her a hardware-first perspective on security: she became convinced that
most threat detection happened too high up the software stack and missed vulnerabilities
closer to the silicon.

In 2012 Okoye left Beacon Semiconductor to found Pinnacle Security, a cybersecurity
company whose flagship product is Sentinel. Sentinel is a threat-detection system that
monitors networks for intrusions and is designed to run efficiently on cloud
infrastructure.

Okoye secured a partnership with Nimbus Systems to offer Sentinel as an add-on service
for the Stratus cloud platform. The partnership positioned Pinnacle Security as the
default cybersecurity option for Nimbus customers and gave it access to the largest
cloud provider in the region.

Leon Ashby has spoken publicly about Okoye's talent, calling her one of the strongest
engineers he worked with at Beacon Semiconductor. Okoye acknowledges that her
hardware-rooted approach to security — watching for threats close to the processor
rather than only at the application layer — grew directly from her years at Beacon.

Pinnacle Security is now the leading cybersecurity provider in the Elmsworth region
and has expanded its Sentinel platform to cover on-premises as well as cloud
environments.
