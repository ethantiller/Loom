# The Stratus Platform

Stratus is the flagship cloud platform developed by Nimbus Systems, the company
founded by Priya Raman and Owen Castellano. It allows companies to run applications
across many data centers without managing physical servers.

Stratus was the product that established Nimbus Systems as the dominant cloud provider
in the Elmsworth region. Its early growth was fueled by funding from Ashby Capital and
Northwind Ventures and by customer demand from companies that could not afford to
build and maintain their own data-center infrastructure.

Several important integrations have extended Stratus beyond pure infrastructure over
the years. After Nimbus Systems acquired Quill in 2016, Quillpad — the note-taking
application made by Marcus Feld — was rebuilt to store documents on Stratus. After the
2018 acquisition of Torch Energy Software, Elena Sorenson's energy-management
technology was folded into Stratus as sustainability features for data-center operators.

Stratus also serves as the delivery backbone for Aperture Media's streaming service and
as the cloud infrastructure on which Halcyon Labs trains and updates the Oracle Engine.
Pinnacle Security's Sentinel product runs on Stratus and is offered to Nimbus customers
as an add-on cybersecurity service.

Cascade Payments integrated with Stratus to offer billing processing as a native cloud
service for Stratus customers. The Atlas Arm's data, processed through the Insight
Suite after Meridian Robotics' acquisition of Vantage Analytics, is also stored on
Stratus by many manufacturing customers.

Stratus is effectively the connective tissue of the Elmsworth technology cluster,
with nearly every significant company in the region running some part of its operations
on the platform.
