# Beacon Semiconductor

Beacon Semiconductor is a chip design company founded in 2001 by Leon Ashby. It
is one of the oldest technology companies in the Elmsworth region and a key
supplier to several others.

Beacon Semiconductor's best-known product is the Photon chip, a processor
optimized for real-time control and machine learning workloads. Meridian Robotics
uses Photon chips inside its Atlas Arm, and Nimbus Systems uses them in parts of
its Stratus data-center hardware.

Leon Ashby is unusual for having a foot in two worlds. In addition to running
Beacon Semiconductor, he founded the venture firm Ashby Capital, which invested in
Nimbus Systems, Halcyon Labs, and Cascade Payments. Grace Okoye, who later founded
the cybersecurity company Pinnacle Security, began her career as an engineer at
Beacon Semiconductor.

Because so many companies depend on the Photon chip, Beacon Semiconductor is
sometimes described as the quiet backbone of the Elmsworth technology cluster.
