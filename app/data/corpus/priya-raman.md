# Priya Raman

Priya Raman is an engineer and entrepreneur who co-founded Nimbus Systems and later
founded Halcyon Labs, making her one of the central figures in the Elmsworth
technology cluster.

Raman began her career in distributed-systems research, where she met Owen Castellano.
In 2004 the two co-founded Nimbus Systems, a cloud infrastructure company whose
Stratus platform became the dominant cloud offering in the Elmsworth region. Nimbus
Systems raised its first outside capital from Ashby Capital, the firm run by Leon
Ashby.

By 2012 Raman felt that her core interest — large language models and AI reasoning
systems — was not a natural fit inside a cloud infrastructure business. She stepped
back from day-to-day operations at Nimbus Systems, remaining on the board, and
founded Halcyon Labs to pursue that research full time. Ashby Capital backed Halcyon
Labs as well, continuing its relationship with Raman.

Halcyon Labs became best known for the Oracle Engine, a large language model licensed
by companies such as Lumen Health. In 2018 Raman transitioned to chair of the Halcyon
Labs board, handing the chief executive role to Mara Lindqvist.

Nadia Cole, who later founded Lumen Health, did her most important AI research under
Raman at Halcyon Labs before departing to start her own company.

Raman is widely credited with seeding the AI branch of the Elmsworth ecosystem, both
through Halcyon Labs directly and through the researchers she trained who went on to
found or lead other companies.
