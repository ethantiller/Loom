# Quill

Quill was a productivity software company founded in 2006 by Marcus Feld. It made
Quillpad, a note-taking and document application that became popular with writers
and students.

Quill was backed early on by Northwind Ventures, the same firm that later
invested in Vantage Analytics and Aperture Media. Theo Vance worked as a product
lead at Quill before leaving to found the payments company Cascade Payments; much
of his interest in simple, friendly software design traces back to his years on
the Quillpad team.

In 2016 Quill was acquired by Nimbus Systems, the cloud company founded by Priya
Raman and Owen Castellano. The acquisition gave Nimbus Systems a consumer-facing
product to pair with its Stratus cloud platform. As part of the deal, Quill's
founder Marcus Feld became the Chief Technology Officer of Nimbus Systems.

After the acquisition, Quillpad was rebuilt to store documents on Stratus, and
the standalone Quill brand was gradually retired in favor of the Nimbus name.
