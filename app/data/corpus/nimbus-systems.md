# Nimbus Systems

Nimbus Systems is a cloud infrastructure company headquartered in Elmsworth. It
was founded in 2004 by Priya Raman and Owen Castellano, two engineers who had
met while working on distributed storage research. Nimbus Systems became known
for its flagship cloud platform, Stratus, which lets companies run applications
across many data centers without managing physical servers.

The company grew quickly on the strength of early funding from Ashby Capital and
a later round led by Northwind Ventures. By 2015 Nimbus Systems was one of the
largest independent cloud providers in the Elmsworth region.

Nimbus Systems is also known for two significant acquisitions. In 2016 it
acquired Quill, the productivity software maker founded by Marcus Feld; after the
deal, Marcus Feld joined Nimbus Systems as its Chief Technology Officer. In 2018
Nimbus Systems acquired Torch Energy Software, the energy-management company
founded by Elena Sorenson, folding its technology into the Stratus platform's
sustainability tools.

Priya Raman left day-to-day work at Nimbus Systems in 2012 to start the AI
research lab Halcyon Labs, though she remained on the Nimbus board. Owen
Castellano departed in 2010 to found Meridian Robotics. Despite their founders
moving on, Nimbus Systems continued to rely on chips supplied by Beacon
Semiconductor and partnered with Pinnacle Security for its cloud defense
offerings.
