# Meridian Robotics

Meridian Robotics is a robotics company founded in 2010 by Owen Castellano after
he left Nimbus Systems, the cloud company he had co-founded with Priya Raman.
Meridian Robotics designs industrial robots for warehouses and factories.

Its signature product is the Atlas Arm, a robotic arm used on assembly lines. The
Atlas Arm depends on high-performance processors supplied by Beacon
Semiconductor, whose Photon chips give the arm its fast motion control.

In 2015 Owen Castellano hired Ravi Anand as chief executive of Meridian Robotics
so that Castellano could concentrate on hardware design. Ravi Anand had earlier
worked at Vantage Analytics, and he brought a strong interest in data to the
robotics company.

That interest shaped Meridian Robotics' most important acquisition: in 2019 it
acquired Vantage Analytics, the analytics firm founded by Dana Whitfield. The
deal let Meridian Robotics combine its robots with Vantage's Insight Suite so
customers could analyze the data their machines generated. After the acquisition
Meridian Robotics marketed itself as an "intelligent automation" company rather
than a pure hardware maker.
