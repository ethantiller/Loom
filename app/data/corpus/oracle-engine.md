# The Oracle Engine

The Oracle Engine is a large language model developed at Halcyon Labs, the AI research
company founded by Priya Raman. It is the most commercially significant product to
emerge from Halcyon Labs and the primary reason the lab earns revenue.

Nadia Cole, who was a researcher at Halcyon Labs before founding Lumen Health,
contributed to early versions of the Oracle Engine. Cole's work focused on applying
the model to structured biomedical data, which laid the groundwork for Lumen Health's
clinical decision support products.

Halcyon Labs licenses the Oracle Engine to other companies rather than developing
end-user products itself. Lumen Health is the most prominent licensee. The licensing
agreement, negotiated and extended under Halcyon Labs CEO Mara Lindqvist, provides
the lab with its largest source of recurring revenue and keeps Halcyon Labs financially
independent of grant funding.

In 2018 Halcyon Labs launched an enterprise API program that allows companies to
fine-tune the Oracle Engine on their own proprietary data. This program opened the
Oracle Engine to a broader set of industries beyond healthcare.

The Oracle Engine is trained and updated on hardware that Halcyon Labs runs on cloud
infrastructure provided by Nimbus Systems. The use of Stratus for model training is
one of several links between Halcyon Labs and its sister company from the Priya Raman
era.

The Oracle Engine is regarded as the technical foundation of the Elmsworth AI cluster,
having either powered or inspired many of the region's subsequent AI-driven products.
