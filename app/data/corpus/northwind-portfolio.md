# Northwind Ventures Portfolio

Northwind Ventures is a venture capital firm based in Elmsworth. It focuses on
software and media companies, making it the counterpart to Ashby Capital's focus on
hardware and infrastructure.

Northwind Ventures built its reputation backing three companies that each became
leaders in their segments. Quill, the productivity software company founded by Marcus
Feld, was one of its earliest investments; Northwind backed Quill when Quillpad was
still a niche product. Vantage Analytics, the data company founded by Dana Whitfield,
was another early investment. Aperture Media, the documentary streaming platform
founded by Sofia Marchetti, rounded out a portfolio that emphasized software and
content.

The firm also participated in a later funding round for Nimbus Systems, giving it
exposure to the region's dominant cloud provider even though Nimbus Systems was
primarily backed by Ashby Capital.

Northwind Ventures invested in Lumen Health when Nadia Cole was raising her first
round, adding a healthcare AI company to a portfolio that had previously been limited
to productivity, analytics, and media.

In the case of Draftwave, Northwind Ventures made a small seed investment, following
its position in Aperture Media and betting that the content supply chain would be
valuable.

Two of Northwind's most prominent exits have been through acquisition: Quill was
acquired by Nimbus Systems in 2016, and Vantage Analytics was acquired by Meridian
Robotics in 2019. Both acquisitions were at prices that analysts considered favorable
for Northwind Ventures.

The firm is one of the two dominant investors in the Elmsworth technology cluster,
alongside Ashby Capital.
