# Lumen Health Clinical Platform

Lumen Health is a medical AI company founded by Nadia Cole in 2015. Its main product
is a clinical decision support platform that helps hospital physicians make diagnostic
and treatment decisions by surfacing relevant research and patient data.

The platform is powered by the Oracle Engine, the large language model developed at
Halcyon Labs. Cole had contributed to early Oracle Engine research while she was a
researcher there under Priya Raman. When she founded Lumen Health, she licensed the
Oracle Engine rather than building a new model, a decision she has described as both
pragmatic and strategic: pragmatic because it reduced time to market, and strategic
because the Oracle Engine's accuracy in Halcyon Labs' own research was already high
enough for clinical applications.

Lumen Health raised early funding from Northwind Ventures. The company runs its
infrastructure on Nimbus Systems' Stratus platform, which provides the data-center
resources needed to serve hospitals around the clock.

The licensing relationship with Halcyon Labs was renewed and expanded in a deal
negotiated with Mara Lindqvist, who became Halcyon Labs' CEO in 2018. The expanded
agreement gives Lumen Health access to fine-tuned versions of the Oracle Engine
trained on anonymized clinical datasets.

Nadia Cole and Priya Raman remain in regular contact, and the Oracle Engine licensing
relationship keeps Lumen Health and Halcyon Labs closely aligned even though they
operate independently. Lumen Health is Halcyon Labs' most significant commercial
customer and the primary proof that the Oracle Engine can function reliably in
high-stakes, regulated environments.
