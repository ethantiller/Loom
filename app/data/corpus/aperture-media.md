# Aperture Media

Aperture Media is a streaming and entertainment technology company founded in
2009 by Sofia Marchetti, who studied at Rowan University. The company operates
StreamHub, a video streaming platform used by independent creators.

Aperture Media raised money from Northwind Ventures, the venture firm that also
backed Quill and Vantage Analytics. Sofia Marchetti built the company around the
idea that small creators needed the same distribution tools as large studios.

In 2015 Aperture Media acquired Draftwave, a design-tools startup founded by
Julian Reyes. The acquisition brought Draftwave's collaborative editing
technology into StreamHub, letting creators design thumbnails and channel art
without leaving the platform. Julian Reyes joined Aperture Media as head of
creator tools after the deal.

Aperture Media is often grouped with the other Elmsworth-area companies because
of its shared Northwind Ventures backing, even though its consumer-media focus
sets it apart from the region's infrastructure and robotics firms.
