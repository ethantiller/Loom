# The Atlas Arm

The Atlas Arm is a robotic arm for factory assembly lines, and the signature product
of Meridian Robotics. It was designed by Owen Castellano, who founded Meridian
Robotics after departing Nimbus Systems, the cloud company he had co-founded with
Priya Raman.

The Atlas Arm uses Photon chips supplied by Beacon Semiconductor to achieve the fast
motion control needed for reliable operation on high-speed production lines. Beacon
Semiconductor is the only chip supplier certified for use inside the Atlas Arm, a
dependency that ties Meridian Robotics closely to Leon Ashby's company.

After Ravi Anand became chief executive of Meridian Robotics in 2015, the company
began exploring how data could make the Atlas Arm more valuable. Anand saw an
opportunity to move Meridian Robotics from a pure hardware company to an intelligent
automation company.

In 2019 Meridian Robotics acquired Vantage Analytics, the company founded by Dana
Whitfield where Anand had spent much of his career. The deal allowed Meridian Robotics
to bundle the Insight Suite with the Atlas Arm, giving factory customers real-time
dashboards showing robot performance, throughput, and maintenance needs.

The Atlas Arm with integrated Insight Suite analytics is now marketed as the core of
Meridian Robotics' intelligent automation offering. Customers store the Atlas Arm's
operational data on Nimbus Systems' Stratus platform, completing the circle between
Meridian Robotics and the cloud company that Owen Castellano helped build before
leaving to start Meridian.

The Atlas Arm is used in manufacturing facilities across the Elmsworth region and in
several international markets.
