# Draftwave and Aperture Media Partnership

Draftwave is a content creation platform founded in 2013 by Jordan Kim, a former
product manager at Aperture Media. Draftwave provides video editing and publishing
tools for independent documentarians and journalists.

Kim had worked under Sofia Marchetti at Aperture Media before leaving to build
Draftwave, which was designed to be the production tool that feeds content into
platforms like Aperture. The idea was to reduce the technical and financial barriers
for independent filmmakers who wanted to create the kind of long-form nonfiction
content that Aperture Media distributes.

Aperture Media and Draftwave entered a formal partnership in 2016, under which
Draftwave users can submit finished projects directly to Aperture Media's programming
review process. The deal made Draftwave the recommended production platform for
Aperture's content contributors.

Sofia Marchetti backed the partnership publicly, noting that the quality of
documentary content depends on the quality of tools available to creators. Draftwave
runs on Nimbus Systems' Stratus platform for its cloud storage and rendering
infrastructure.

Northwind Ventures, which had invested in Aperture Media, also made a small investment
in Draftwave, reflecting confidence that the content supply chain that feeds Aperture's
library could itself be a valuable business.

Jordan Kim has described building Draftwave as a direct extension of lessons learned
at Aperture Media under Marchetti's leadership — specifically the belief that long-form
content requires patient, tool-enabled craftsmanship rather than speed and scale.
