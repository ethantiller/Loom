# Nadia Cole

Nadia Cole is a researcher and entrepreneur who founded Lumen Health, a medical AI
company that applies the Oracle Engine developed at Halcyon Labs.

Cole began her career as an AI researcher at Halcyon Labs, the research company
founded by Priya Raman. Her work at Halcyon Labs focused on applying large language
models to structured data, and she became one of the leading contributors to early
versions of the Oracle Engine.

In 2015 Cole left Halcyon Labs to found Lumen Health, bringing deep familiarity with
the Oracle Engine with her. Rather than rebuild a foundation model from scratch, Lumen
Health licensed the Oracle Engine from Halcyon Labs to power its clinical decision
support tools. That licensing relationship remains the financial backbone of Halcyon
Labs' revenue.

Lumen Health raised early funding from Northwind Ventures, the same firm that had
backed Quill and Vantage Analytics. Cole has spoken about how Priya Raman mentored
her both as a researcher and as someone who would eventually run an organization.

Cole serves on the advisory board of several Elmsworth-area universities and advocates
for responsible AI use in healthcare. She and Priya Raman remain in contact: Lumen
Health's dependence on the Oracle Engine keeps the two companies aligned even though
they operate independently.

Cole is credited with bringing Halcyon Labs' AI research into a regulated, real-world
industry, demonstrating that the Oracle Engine is robust enough for high-stakes
clinical settings.
