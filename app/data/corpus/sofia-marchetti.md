# Sofia Marchetti

Sofia Marchetti is a media entrepreneur who founded Aperture Media, the streaming
company backed by Northwind Ventures.

Marchetti began her career in digital publishing, editing technology journalism for
regional outlets before concluding that the shift from text to video was irreversible.
In 2009 she founded Aperture Media to build a subscription streaming platform focused
on documentary and long-form nonfiction content. She believed that general-purpose
streaming services would crowd out substantive documentary programming in favor of
mass entertainment, and that a niche platform could build a loyal, paying audience.

Northwind Ventures invested in Aperture Media in 2010, joining a portfolio that also
included Quill and Vantage Analytics. The investment let Marchetti expand Aperture's
content library and build a mobile application.

Aperture Media relies on Nimbus Systems' Stratus platform for its video delivery
infrastructure. The relationship began early: Stratus was one of the few cloud
platforms at the time that could handle the storage and streaming demands of
high-definition video at competitive costs.

Marchetti has spoken about how the documentary genre requires patience — both in
production and in audience building — which is the opposite of the growth-at-all-costs
philosophy common in venture-backed startups. Aperture Media has grown steadily rather
than explosively, which she attributes to the depth of its content rather than
marketing spend.

Aperture Media is now one of Northwind Ventures' longest-held investments, and
Marchetti remains its founder and chief executive.
