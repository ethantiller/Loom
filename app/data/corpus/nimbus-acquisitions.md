# Nimbus Systems Acquisitions

Nimbus Systems, the cloud company co-founded by Priya Raman and Owen Castellano, has
made two significant acquisitions that expanded its Stratus platform from pure
infrastructure to a suite of software services.

The first acquisition was Quill in 2016. Quill was a productivity software company
founded by Marcus Feld, whose Quillpad application had built a loyal following among
writers and students. Nimbus Systems bought Quill to give Stratus a consumer-facing
product. As part of the deal, Marcus Feld became Chief Technology Officer of Nimbus
Systems. Quillpad was rebuilt to store documents on Stratus, and the standalone Quill
brand was retired over the following years.

The second acquisition was Torch Energy Software in 2018. Torch was founded by Elena
Sorenson, who had also served on the Nimbus Systems board for several years before
the deal. Nimbus Systems folded Torch's energy-management technology into Stratus as
sustainability features, giving data-center operators tools to monitor and reduce
power consumption. After the acquisition, Sorenson stayed on as a sustainability
adviser to Nimbus Systems.

Both acquisitions reflect a deliberate strategy to move Nimbus Systems from
infrastructure-only to a platform that bundles infrastructure with productivity and
efficiency software. Marcus Feld, as CTO, played a central role in integrating both
acquired companies' technology into Stratus.

The acquisitions also deepened the web of relationships connecting the Elmsworth
technology cluster, bringing Feld's network from the Quill era and Sorenson's network
from the energy sector into the Nimbus Systems orbit.
