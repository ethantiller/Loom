# Quillpad

Quillpad is a note-taking and document application originally built by Quill, the
productivity software company founded by Marcus Feld. It became one of the most
popular writing tools among students and writers in the Elmsworth region before
expanding to a national audience.

Feld designed Quillpad around the belief that writing software had become too
complicated. He stripped the interface down to a focused writing view with minimal
menus and automatic saving, at a time when most document software was loaded with
rarely-used formatting options.

Theo Vance worked on the Quillpad product team at Quill under Marcus Feld. Vance has
said that designing Quillpad taught him that users trust software that does fewer
things well, a lesson he applied when designing the interfaces for Cascade Payments.

Northwind Ventures, which had invested in Quill, included Quillpad's growth as a
central part of its due diligence on the company. The subscription model Feld chose
for Quillpad later became a model for other Northwind-backed software companies.

When Nimbus Systems acquired Quill in 2016, Quillpad was rebuilt to store documents
on the Stratus platform. The move gave Quillpad seamless synchronization across
devices, but also tied Quillpad's future to Nimbus Systems' infrastructure decisions.
Feld oversaw this migration as Nimbus Systems' Chief Technology Officer.

The standalone Quill brand was retired after the acquisition, but Quillpad continued
as a product inside Nimbus Systems. Many users were unaware of the ownership change
because Feld insisted on preserving the product's identity and interface during the
transition.
