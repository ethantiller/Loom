# Halcyon Labs

Halcyon Labs is an artificial intelligence research company founded in 2012 by
Priya Raman, who had previously co-founded the cloud company Nimbus Systems. She
started Halcyon Labs to focus on large language models and reasoning systems,
work she felt did not fit inside an infrastructure business.

Halcyon Labs is best known for the Oracle Engine, a large language model that
other companies license to power their own products. Lumen Health, the medical
technology company founded by Nadia Cole, is one of the most prominent users of
the Oracle Engine.

The lab was funded primarily by Ashby Capital, the venture firm run by Leon
Ashby, which had also backed Nimbus Systems in its early days. This shared
investor tie is often cited as an example of how Elmsworth's technology companies
are financially interconnected.

In 2018 Priya Raman stepped back from the chief executive role at Halcyon Labs
and Mara Lindqvist became CEO. Priya Raman remained chair of the board. Nadia
Cole worked as a researcher at Halcyon Labs before leaving to found Lumen Health,
carrying her experience with the Oracle Engine into the health sector.
