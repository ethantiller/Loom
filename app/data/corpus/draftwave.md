# Draftwave

Draftwave was a design-tools startup founded in 2014 by Julian Reyes. It made
collaborative editing software that let teams design images and layouts together
in a web browser.

Draftwave was small but influential, admired for the smoothness of its real-time
collaboration. That reputation attracted the attention of Aperture Media, the
streaming company founded by Sofia Marchetti.

In 2015 Aperture Media acquired Draftwave and integrated its editing technology
into the StreamHub platform, giving creators built-in design tools. Julian Reyes
joined Aperture Media as head of creator tools following the acquisition.

Although the Draftwave brand disappeared after the deal, its collaborative editing
engine remained a core part of StreamHub, and Julian Reyes continued to shape how
creators design content on Aperture Media's platform.
