# Elena Sorenson

Elena Sorenson is a software entrepreneur who founded Torch Energy Software and later
became an adviser to Nimbus Systems after that company acquired her startup.

Sorenson founded Torch Energy Software in 2007. The company built tools that helped
large buildings and factories track and reduce their energy consumption. Before
founding Torch, Sorenson worked as an engineer at regional utilities and developed a
strong interest in data-driven approaches to energy efficiency.

Sorenson was well connected in the Elmsworth technology community and served as a
board member of Nimbus Systems for several years, giving her unusual insight into how
the dominant cloud provider thought about its infrastructure costs. That relationship
eventually helped bring the two companies together.

In 2018 Nimbus Systems acquired Torch Energy Software. Owen Castellano, who had
co-founded Nimbus and remained influential on the board, supported the deal. Marcus
Feld, serving as Chief Technology Officer, managed the technical integration. Nimbus
Systems folded Torch's energy-management tools into the Stratus platform as
sustainability features, marketing them to data-center operators trying to cut power
costs.

After the acquisition Sorenson stayed on as a sustainability adviser to Nimbus Systems
rather than taking a full-time role. She has said that she was more interested in
continuing to develop energy-efficiency techniques than in managing a large team
inside an infrastructure company.

Torch Energy Software's technology lives on inside Stratus, even though the standalone
company no longer exists. Sorenson continues to advise on Nimbus Systems' environmental
commitments.
