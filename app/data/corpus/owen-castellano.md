# Owen Castellano

Owen Castellano is an engineer and entrepreneur who co-founded Nimbus Systems and
later founded Meridian Robotics, two of the most influential companies in the
Elmsworth technology cluster.

Castellano met Priya Raman during distributed-storage research, and together they
founded Nimbus Systems in 2004. The company grew into the dominant cloud provider in
the Elmsworth region, powered by its Stratus platform. In 2010, after six years
building Nimbus Systems, Castellano departed to found Meridian Robotics, a robotics
company focused on industrial automation.

At Meridian Robotics, Castellano concentrated on hardware design. He built the Atlas
Arm, a robotic arm for factory assembly lines that uses Photon chips supplied by
Beacon Semiconductor. To let a professional manager grow the business, Castellano
hired Ravi Anand as chief executive in 2015. Anand had previously worked at Vantage
Analytics, and his data background shaped Meridian Robotics' 2019 acquisition of
Vantage Analytics, which let the company bundle data analysis tools with its robots.

Castellano is admired in the Elmsworth community as a builder rather than a promoter.
He is known to prefer working on hardware schematics to attending investor events.
After the Vantage Analytics acquisition he withdrew from management to focus again on
new product design.

His two companies — Nimbus Systems and Meridian Robotics — together account for a
large share of the Elmsworth region's technology employment, and both depend on chips
from Beacon Semiconductor, founded by Leon Ashby.
