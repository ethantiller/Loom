# Marcus Feld

Marcus Feld is a software developer and technology executive who founded Quill and
later became Chief Technology Officer of Nimbus Systems.

Feld founded Quill in 2006 as a productivity software company. Quill's main product
was Quillpad, a note-taking and document application that became popular with writers
and students. The company was backed by Northwind Ventures, the same venture firm
that later invested in Vantage Analytics and Aperture Media.

Theo Vance worked under Feld as a product lead at Quill before leaving to found
Cascade Payments. Feld's emphasis on simple, intuitive design had a lasting influence
on Vance's approach to product development.

In 2016 Nimbus Systems, the cloud company co-founded by Priya Raman and Owen
Castellano, acquired Quill. The acquisition gave Nimbus a consumer-facing product to
pair with its Stratus cloud platform. As part of the deal, Feld joined Nimbus Systems
as its Chief Technology Officer.

As CTO, Feld oversaw the rebuilding of Quillpad to store documents on the Stratus
platform. He also guided Nimbus Systems through its 2018 acquisition of Torch Energy
Software, integrating Elena Sorenson's energy-management tools into Stratus.

Feld describes the acquisition as bittersweet: he was proud of what Quill had built
but recognized that Nimbus Systems could give Quillpad the infrastructure it needed
to scale. The Quill brand was gradually retired after the acquisition, but Quillpad
survived inside Nimbus Systems under the Nimbus name.
